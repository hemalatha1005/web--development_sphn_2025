Temples

Tirupati Venkateswara Temple

Tirupati Venkateswara Temple, often called the Temple of Seven Hills, is one of the most visited pilgrimage centers in the world and the richest temple in India. Situated atop the Seshachalam Hills in Andhra Pradesh, it is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Vishnu. The temple’s gold-plated tower, known as Ananda Nilayam, houses the main idol adorned with jewels and flowers. Devotees from everywhere come to offer hair and riches as prayers. The temple’s beautiful Dravidian architecture and spiritual atmosphere make it a special destination for millions of people[web:21][web:35].

Arunachalam (Arunachaleswarar) Temple

Arunachaleswarar Temple, located in Tiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu, is one of the largest and most sacred Shiva temples in India. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of Arunachaleswarar (Agni Lingam), representing the fire element among the Pancha Bhoota Stalas. Pilgrims perform Giripradakshina — walking barefoot around the sacred Arunachala Hill — which is believed to cleanse sins and bring spiritual blessings. The temple features magnificent gopurams, vast courtyards, and impressive Dravidian architecture. Devotees visit this holy site seeking liberation and divine peace[web:34][web:38].

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple in New Delhi, India, is shaped like a white lotus flower. Built in 1986, this Baháʼí House of Worship welcomes people of all religions. Its tranquil gardens and peaceful ambiance make the Lotus Temple a popular site for meditation and spiritual reflection[web:11].

Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath Temple is located high up in the Himalayas and is among the most sacred shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. With its ancient stone walls and scenic mountain backdrop, the temple attracts thousands of pilgrims every year, especially during the Char Dham Yatra[web:4].
